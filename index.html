Featured Projects

Published

Graph-Based Modeling for Ion Activity Coefficient Prediction

Developed a novel GNN model to predict ion activity in polymeric ion-exchange membranes, combining molecular dynamics simulations with graph neural networks.

Technologies: Python, PyTorch, GCN, Molecular Dynamics simulations, Saliency analysis
Results: Both models achieved high predictive accuracy (R² > 0.98 for train and test sets). The MD descriptors model demonstrated superior robustness across diverse hyperparameter configurations.
Publication GitHub
Ongoing

Graph-Based Models for Log CMC Prediction in Surfactants

Comparative study of three graph-based machine learning models (GCN, GAT, PharmHGT) for predicting critical micelle concentration (LogCMC) of surfactants.

Technologies: Python, PyTorch, Graph Neural Networks (GCN, GAT), Transformer-Based Models (PharmHGT)
Impact: Identifying the most suitable graph-based model for predicting surfactant properties, enabling more efficient design of surfactants for detergents, emulsifiers, and drug delivery.
GitHub Manuscript
Ongoing

VAE-Assisted Data Augmentation for GNNs

Implemented Variational Autoencoders to augment limited molecular data for Graph Neural Network training in low-data regimes, improving model generalization.

Technologies: Python, PyTorch, RDKit, GAT, MPNN, GCN, Variational Autoencoders
Impact: Enables accurate predictions in low-data regimes, significantly improving model generalization for molecular property prediction tasks.
GitHub Results
Ongoing

ML-Enhanced Surfactant Adsorption Prediction

Developed a pipeline combining machine learning and molecular dynamics to predict the standard Gibbs free energy of adsorption for surfactants at interfaces.

Technologies: Python, PyTorch, RDKit, GROMACS, Free Energy Perturbation (FEP), Graph Neural Networks
Potential Impact: Enables accurate, scalable prediction of surfactant adsorption behavior, accelerating design of efficient surfactants for detergency, emulsification, and pharmaceutical applications.
GitHub Methods
